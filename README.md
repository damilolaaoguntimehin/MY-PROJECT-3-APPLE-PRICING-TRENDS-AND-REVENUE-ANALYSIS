Discovering $1.33M in Apple Revenue Patterns: A detailed analysis


Introduction


Every dataset tells a story - if you know where to look.

In this project, I set out to uncover the patterns hidden within an Apple sales dataset, using Microsoft Excel as my primary analytical tool. The objective was clear: to analyze pricing trends, understand revenue distribution, and uncover actionable insights that can guide business decisions.

In essence, this analysis answers a simple but powerful question:

"What drives revenue in Apple product sales across different markets, categories, and time periods?"

To achieve this, I worked with structured sales data and applied Excel techniques using Pivot Tables, calculated fields, and interactive dashboards to transform raw numbers into meaningful insights.

Data Story

Behind every dashboard lies a journey - and this data was no different.

The dataset represents Apple product sales across multiple countries, categories, and market segments. Each row captures the variables: Independent variables: (order Id, time period, apple variety, country, quality grade and market type) while the dependent variables includes quantity sold(kg), price per kg($) and revenue generated($).

The data reflects a retail/supermarket industry context, where pricing and demand fluctuate based on seasonality and consumer preference and the analysis is valuable for Business managers, Sales and marketing teams and retail strategists providing insights that can optimize pricing, inventory, and regional focus.

Pre-Analysis: First Glimpses into the Data


Even before deep analysis, patterns began to emerge;

Seasonality was obvious, with noticeable fluctuations in monthly sales

Certain product categories appeared to dominate in volume

Revenue seemed unevenly distributed across countries

These early signals raised important questions:

Why do some months outperform others?

Which products truly drive revenue?

Are certain markets more profitable than others?

In-Analysis Discoveries


Using Pivot Tables and charts, I found deeper insights

 - Total revenue generated for the year is $1.33M
 
 - October recorded the highest sales, confirming strong seasonal demand
 
 - China emerged as the top revenue-generating country, indicating market dominance
 
 - Supermarkets contributed the highest share of revenue, outperforming other market types
 
 - Fuji apples led in quantity sold, showing strong consumer preference
 
- Products priced between 2000–3000 recorded the highest sales volume, indicating this range as the most attractive price point for customers.
- 
- In terms of product quality, Standard-grade items generated the highest total revenue, outperforming both Premium and Economy categories. This suggests that customers are highly budget conscious rather than just premium positioning although they all contribute significantly to total revenue.

Post-Analysis and Recommendations

Insights are only valuable when they drive action
 
 - Focus on high-performing markets on peak months: 
 
Increase marketing efforts and inventory procurement for October across top markets (China & Germany) and expand operations in these countries.

- Rebalance variety mix:
- 
Increase Gala and Pink Lady procurement for Supermarket channels ahead of October. They deliver better revenue per kg.

 - Leverage on best-selling products:
 
 Prioritize Fuji apples in promotions and supply chains
 
 - Optimize pricing around the 2000–3000 range:
 
Since this band drives the most transactions, the business should prioritize stocking and promoting products within this price range to maximize sales volume.

Balance between premium and economy products to maximize revenue

-  Leverage on channel options:
  
Online Store is within 2.6% of Supermarket revenue. Shift marketing spend to online in June to August to flatten seasonality.

 - Strengthen the Standard-grade product strategy:
 
Increase the availability of Standard-grade items by bundling or slight upselling.

In conclusion, I learned  that - Market type plays a crucial role in revenue generation, Consumer preferences vary significantly across regions and strategic timing can significantly impact sales outcomes.

This project is more than just numbers, it's a reflection of how data can guide smarter decisions.
